BYU Racing - Formula Electric

In May 2022, me and 2 other guys decided that we wanted to build an electric racecar. We started the Formula Electric team on campus and began working on figuring out adminstration of the club, recruiting new members, and learning about racecar dynamics. We quickly found more passionate people to join the team and we broke it up into initially 6 engineering subteams. Since then, I have been the Lead Electronics Engineer for the team.

The Electronics team is in charge of all of the low voltage circuits and the software. We started by doing our research and deciding to use Teensy Microcontrollers for all of the processing that the car would be doing and to create custom software that would allow for our sensor package to be easily adaptible. I designed the structure of our Object-Oriented custom software and led the team as we developed each class and tested it. We took the car to the competition in Michigan in June 2024 and after 3 days of hard work were able to pass all technical inspections. The car was not able to pass the rain test so it was unable to drive in the dynamic events but, I am very proud of the team and the work each member put into it.

This team has helped me to develop many technical as well as soft skills. As an example of some of the soft skills that I was able to develop, while at the competition, a purely analog safety circuit called the BSPD was found out to be faulty. In the first 3 days of the competition I learned how to design, test, and build a replacement BSPD without having prior experience. I am confident in my problem solving abilities and in my ability to acquire necessary skills at a very quick pace.
