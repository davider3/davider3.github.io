BYU Racing - Formula Electric

In May 2022, me and 2 other guys decided that we wanted to build an electric racecar. We started the Formula Electric team on campus and began working on figuring out adminstration of the club, recruiting new members, and learning about racecar dynamics. We quickly found more passionate people to join the team and we broke it up into initially 6 engineering subteams. Since then, I have been the Lead Electronics Engineer for the team.

The Electronics team is in charge of all of the low voltage circuits and the software. We started by doing our research and deciding to use Teensy Microcontrollers for all of the processing that the car would be doing and to create custom software that would allow for our sensor package to be easily adaptible. I designed the structure of our Object-Oriented custom software and led the team as we developed each class and tested it. We are in the process of preforming vehicle tests collecting data from the different sensors. This data is analyzed with the other engineers as well as the drivers in preparation for competition in June 2024!
